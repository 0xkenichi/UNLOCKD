The Illiquidity Paradox

Vesting and lockups protect alignment — but trap real economic value.

  • Ownership is clear (smart contracts assign tokens)
  • Value is observable (oracles reflect market price)
  • Utility is zero — non-transferable during lockup

Gaps in existing solutions

  • Aave / Compound / Maker — require liquid collateral; vested positions can't be deposited
  • Centralized lenders — custody, KYC, legal enforcement
  • OTC & SecondSwap — forced sales at 20–70% discounts; no borrowing primitive

The Solution

Borrow against escrowed claim rights — not the tokens themselves.

PV = Q × P × D

Q = quantity • P = oracle price • D = composite discount (time, volatility, liquidity, unlock impact)

  • Non-custodial — users keep wallet control; only claim rights escrowed
  • On-chain settlement at unlock — no courts, no custodians
  • Conservative LTV (20–40%) — Monte Carlo–calibrated for lender safety
  • Optional identity layer — better terms for verified borrowers

Transformation, not iteration. A foundational primitive for DAOs, startups, and contributors.

Slide 4

How It Works

1

Escrow — Borrower escrows vesting claim via adapter (OZ, Sablier, etc.)

2

Valuation — Engine computes DPV + max borrow (LTV cap)

3

Loan — LoanManager issues USDC from lending pool

4

Repay — Borrower can repay anytime pre-unlock

5

Settlement — At unlock: full repay → release tokens; partial/default → seize → liquidate

Optional: Debt-Free Exit

Dutch, English, or Sealed Bid auctions let users sell claim rights for immediate liquidity — no loan, no repayment.
