# Go-To-Market Pipeline

This document defines the execution plan to move from testnet proof to repeatable market adoption.

## Target Customers

Primary:

- DAO contributors with vesting tokens
- Early-stage startup teams with lockups
- Treasury managers seeking capital efficiency

Secondary:

- Yield-seeking lenders/funds
- Market makers for claim-right liquidity paths

## Distribution Model

- B2B2C: partner DAOs/protocols onboard their contributors and treasuries
- Direct product: self-serve borrower and lender onboarding for open pools
- Ecosystem channels: grants, hackathons, and protocol integration partners

## Pipeline Stages

1. Prospecting
   - Build partner target list by chain/ecosystem
   - Prioritize by vesting volume and treasury activity
2. Qualification
   - Confirm vesting contract compatibility
   - Evaluate legal/compliance constraints
   - Validate liquidity partner interest
3. Pilot Design
   - Define pilot collateral set
   - Define pool risk parameters and reporting expectations
4. Pilot Launch
   - Activate testnet pilot with named counterparties
   - Track conversion and repayment metrics
5. Expansion
   - Convert pilots to production pools
   - Add additional DAOs and lender cohorts

## 90-Day Milestones

- 15+ qualified partner conversations
- 5 pilot-ready counterparties
- 2 signed pilot LOIs
- 1 live named pilot with published KPI report

## Sales and Success Artifacts

- Partner one-pager (problem, solution, risk controls)
- Pilot playbook (technical + legal checklist)
- KPI dashboard link and monthly report template
- Post-pilot case study template

## Core Messaging

- Unlock non-transferable value without breaking vesting alignment
- Borrow against discounted present value with explicit risk controls
- Non-custodial architecture with governance and auditable parameter changes

## Execution Risks and Mitigations

- Risk: partner legal hesitation
  - Mitigation: publish compliance policy options and pool-level controls
- Risk: insufficient lender depth
  - Mitigation: reserve-backed bootstrap pools and dedicated lender onboarding
- Risk: low borrower conversion
  - Mitigation: simplify onboarding, prefilled collateral valuation, guided eligibility

## Ownership and Cadence

- Weekly GTM standup (pipeline review)
- Bi-weekly pilot readiness review with risk committee
- Monthly board/investor update with KPI movement
